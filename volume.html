Transaction fees vary significantly during network congestion. For instance, Bitcoin fees surged to $15 per transaction in November 2023, while Ethereum fees often spiked above $10 during heavy demand. The research by Easley et al. shows that longer waiting times correlate with fewer zero-fee transactions‚Äîdemonstrating a market-based fee dynamic.

Such volatility in fees reflects the limited throughput of blockchains and auction-style fee markets. High fees can price out smaller users and have led to scaling efforts such as Ethereum Layer-2 solutions and Bitcoin Lightning Network.
